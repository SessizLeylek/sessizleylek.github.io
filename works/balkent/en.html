This city first appeared in September 2023 as a creative Minecraft server where my cousin and my second cousin built a few buildings together. It consisted of two wooden buildings facing each other along a street they called Güllübağ Street, and skyscrapers next to them. When I joined the server, I found this arrangement a bit illogical and started writing a story for the city to explain why two completely separate residentials would stand side-by-side. We divided the city into two sides, thus separating the skyscrapers from the wooden buildings. According to the story, a tectonic reactivation in a nearby mountain caused a river of lava to form, so the city could only be built east of the lava river until modern times. The cooling of the lava river over time made it possible to build buildings near it. Therefore, the east side of the city mostly contained wooden medieval houses, while skyscrapers rose on the west side.
After that, of course, we continued working on the server together for a while, but I was spending a lot of time in the city and they stopped working on the server. So, in early 2024, I saved the world to my computer and we shut down the server. Since then, I've continued to grow the city, usually thanks to the hypnotic effect of mindlessly building structures when I'm too stressed or overwhelmed to do anything else.
The city is located in an alternate history I created, in the Golden Horde Republic, on the northern shore of Lake Issyk-Kul, slightly west of where Cholpon-Ata is today. The idea of setting the city in a country that was a continuation of the old Kipchak Khaganates was there from the beginning. Initially, we placed the city on the northern shore of the Caspian Sea, somewhere between Saray, the capital of the Golden Horde, and Atyrau. After learning that there was no mountain range north of the Caspian Sea, we were forced to move the city north of Lake Issyk-Kul. Then I decided to include this city in a universe containing some of my earlier games that never progressed beyond the concept stage. These canceled games depicted a post-war period between America and an Eastern power in the future. I worked on the story and history a bit more and evolved it into a scenario where, as a result of bureaucratic changes in the 15th-16th century, the Golden Horde did not collapse but underwent a regime change, the Soviet Union did not come into being, and instead the Golden Horde Republic rose much more stably. Balkent is currently a 21st-century city in the Golden Horde Republic. The story will naturally continue with increased rivalry between America and the Golden Horde Republic, but that doesn't concern Balkent at the moment.
